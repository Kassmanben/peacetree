About Me

After 25 years of being in practice, I am still as passionate as I was on my first day as a therapist. I love what I do and enjoy helping others remove blocks that hinder their ability to make positive changes in their lives. As a Colorado Registered Psychotherapist with a Master's degree in Counseling Psychology, I have had the opportunity to work in a number of different settings.

Beyond my counseling practice, I am also a speaker and educator for various non-profits and universities. I have helped develop sustainable programs with K-12 schools addressing such issues as bullying, healthy relationships, sexual harassment & assault and substance abuse.

I am especially proud to have co-created a local high school Peer Counseling program. We train and supervise select juniors and seniors to provide services like: one-on-one counseling, peer conflict mediation, educational campaigns and annual Diversity Day and Day Without Hate conferences.

Clients have described me as empathetic, insightful, humorous and resourceful. I take a comprehensive approach to therapy, working with my clients to find the best fit. There is not "one quick fix" that works for every person.

My role is to provide a safe environment to work through difficult emotions and stumbling blocks, and to help you see your way to personal growth.

In my time off, you can find me reading, cooking, traveling, taking in theatre and music performances and enjoying any number of outdoor activities.
